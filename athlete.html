Introduction

Milkha Singh (20 November 1929 - 18 June 2021) also known as 'The Flying Sikh' was an Indian track and field sprinter who entered in the athletics while serving in the Indian Army. He is the only athlete to win gold at 400 metres at the Asian Games as well as the Commonwealth Games. He also won gold medals in the Asian Games in 1958 and 1962. He represented India at the:

  • Summer Olympics in Melbourne in 1956
  • The Summer Olympics in Rome in 1960
  • The Summer Olympics in Tokyo in 1964.
    •
For his athletic accomplishments, he received the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honor.Singh has gone from humble origins, including being abandoned and uprooted during India's Partition, to becoming a national athletic hero. Singh was referred to as "The finest athlete India has ever produced" by columnist Rohit Brijnath in 2008.
(Wikipedia, 2021)

International Career

Milkha Singh started his international career from the 1956 Melbourne Olympics. He represented India in the 200m and 400m category. Although he could not get past the heat stages, meeting champion Charles Jenkins at the games inspired him greatly.

  • In 1958, he won 2 gold medals in the 200m and 400m events at the Asian Games. He then won a gold medal in the 400m competition at the British Empire and Commonwealth Games, which were held the same year, with a time of 46.6 seconds. This made him the first gold medalist at the Commonwealth Games from independent India.
  • In 1960, after his successful race against Abdul Khaliq in Pakistan, a post-race comment by General Ayub Khan led him to acquire the title of The Flying Sikh.
  • In the 1960 olympics, running at the 400m event, he was leading till the 200m mark but he slowed down and ended up finishing fourth. He finished the race with a timing of 45.73 seconds, setting a National Record that stood for 40 years.
  • In the 1962 Asian Games, Milkha Singh won 2 gold medals, one in 400m and the other in 4 x 100m relay.

Later Life

Following his achievement in 1958, Singh received the Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian honor. He declined an offer of the Arjuna Award from the Indian government in 2001, saying that it was meant to honor youthful athletes rather than people like him. He also believed that it was wrong to offer the Award to those who had little to no prominent engagement in competitive sports. He said that the award had lost its meaning and that "I have been clubbed with athletes who are nowhere near the level that I had achieved." In 2014, he described his experience in a college in Goa by saying, "Awards today are given out like 'prasad' in a shrine. Why should someone be honored if they haven't met the criteria for the award? After receiving the Padma Shri, I turned down the Arjuna that was presented to me. It was comparable to receiving an SSC certificate after earning a master's degree."
(Wikipedia, 2021)

Personal Life

Singh resided in Chandigarh as of 2012. In Ceylon in 1955, he fell in love with Nirmal Saini, a former captain of the Indian women's volleyball team. The two were wed in 1962 and had three daughters in addition to a son, golfer Jeev Milkha Singh. They adopted Havildar Bikram Singh's seven-year-old son in 1999 after he lost his life in the Battle of Tiger Hill.
(Wikipedia, 2021)
